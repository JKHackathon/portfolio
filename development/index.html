Development

As a front-end developer, you often notice little features or behaviors on web apps that either spark a bit of joy or drive you absolutely crazy. When forming your own creation, you must strive to “steal” others’ good designs and make sure to steer clear from making the same mistakes.

The Hunger to Find Good Features

This specific project focused on creating an aggregator of sorts that allows for a favoriting function, along with different filters. Given my love of food and the ever-present struggle of finding easy meals to cook, I decided to create a recipe app.

Actually finding web apps with unique favoriting and sorting functionality proved rather challenging. Most are blocked behind login pages, and a lot are simply far too similar. After much searching, I decided upon Reddit, Spotify, and EdStem. All web apps I am well-familiar with while each serves a very different purpose.

Each app indicates their saved items differently: Reddit having a separate tab for them, Spotify grouping saved songs in playlists, and EdStem by simply showing a star next to favorited posts. However, unlike the others, Reddit does not actually overtly indicate if a post is saved, requiring you to open up a menu to check its status. This simply indicates that saving posts is not a priority feature to Reddit. Furthermore, Reddit’s section for saved posts does not particularly stand out as it is amid many other categories, as you can see:

Both Spotify and Reddit had a nice feature where an alert banner notified you of your action. Spotify even took it a step further by allowing you to change that action by saving a song to a different playlist. While I am a big fan of quick undo buttons, I didn’t feel it would apply to my application.

Edstem’s downside is that its filtering functionality is rather limited in terms of combining filters, which in my experience has often made it difficult to find specific posts.

Overall, there are many different interesting design choices made by these companies in terms of the features they included and how they would portray them to users. While some served to simplify interactions, others, in my opinion, lacked intentional thought leading to a hindered experience.

Whipping Up My Creation

After completing my research, it was time to create my recipe app. I decided to create cards for each recipe that included the key information in a relatively straightforward format. All the filtering categories are clearly indicated within this card, with the save button standing out due to its bright color. I chose to store all saved recipes in their own tab as this allowed for a consistent feel whether looking at all recipes or your saved recipes.

Upon saving a recipe, a alert pops up just to make it even more clear to a user that they completed an action. As mentioned previously, I thought of including an “undo” option within the popup, however, I decided against it as the “save” button transitions to an “unsave” button which allows for users to easily change their mind.
